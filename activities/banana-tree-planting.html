Banana Tree Planting at Daro's Enclave

Plant a Tree, Leave a Legacy

Why You'll Love It

← Back

At Daro's Enclave, we believe in creating memories that last beyond your stay. Our Banana Tree Planting experience gives you the chance to leave a lasting, living footprint in Sri Lanka.

It's more than just planting a tree — it's a connection with the land, the community, and the environment. Guests of all ages love getting their hands in the soil, learning about local traditions, and knowing that the tree they plant will continue to thrive for years to come.

  • A hands-on eco-friendly activity for guests
  • Learn about the importance of banana trees in Sri Lankan culture
  • A symbolic gesture of giving back to nature
  • Fun and meaningful for families, couples, and solo travelers

Plan Your Visit

Location & Distance

The activity takes place right at Daro's Enclave, in our garden space dedicated to guest-planted banana trees.

Best Time to Visit

  • Anytime during your stay
  • Morning or late afternoon is recommended for cooler weather

What to Expect

  • Guided session on how to plant and care for your banana tree
  • Insight into how bananas are used in Sri Lankan cuisine and daily life
  • A photo opportunity with your tree — a memory to cherish and revisit on future stays
  • Duration: 30–45 minutes

Who It's For

  • Families: Kids love this hands-on, nature-based activity
  • Eco-Conscious Travelers: Contribute positively to the environment
  • Couples: A symbolic way to create something lasting together
  • Cultural Travelers: Learn about the role of banana trees in local life

Insider Tips

  • Wear comfortable clothes and shoes you don't mind getting a little muddy
  • Ask your host to explain how bananas feature in Sri Lankan dishes and rituals
  • Take a photo with your planted tree to remember your contribution
  • Water your tree during your stay to help it settle

Explore Nearby

  • Guided Cooking Experience at Daro's Enclave: Use banana leaves and fruits in authentic Sri Lankan dishes
  • Traditional Games of Sri Lanka: Continue your cultural exploration with family-friendly activities
  • Lagoon Safari: Combine your eco-experience with a nature adventure

FAQs

A: Not at all! Our team will guide you step by step.

Q: How long does it take to plant a tree?

A: About 30–45 minutes, making it a perfect short activity to fit into your day.

Q: Can children join this activity?

A: Absolutely — kids love it, and it's safe, fun, and educational.
